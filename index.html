CLOSE READINGS

September 24–November 6, 2022

Opening Reception: Sat. September 24, 5–7PM

Smack Mellon is thrilled to announce the launch of the new exhibition series Close Readings. Once per year, Smack Mellon will present a new commission by one under-recognized, early to mid-career artist in Gallery One along with an accompanying exhibition in Gallery Two that uses the central commission as a curatorial framework. This program extends our support for interdisciplinary artists by building conversations around their practices in addition to facilitating the realization of ambitious, site-specific projects.

Luba Drozd

The Tenacity of a Fluid Trace

EXHIBITION
Gallery One

In her first major solo exhibition in NYC, Luba Drozd presents a sprawling installation comprising construction materials and motors attached to piano strings and tuned to various frequencies, using the tension between these materials to produce a complex sonic landscape that unfolds across the main gallery space. The installation explores links between empathy, humanity, and the primordial composition of the universe through the artist’s deep investigation of matter.

Image credit: Luba Drozd, The Tenacity of a Fluid Trace (detail), 2022. Site specific sound and animation installation, dimensions variable. Courtesy of the Artist.

Fire is in all Bodies

EXHIBITION
Gallery Two

Smack Mellon presents a group exhibition featuring works by Leah Beeferman (with soundtracks by Byron Westbrook, Olli Aarni, and Bryce Hackford), Mimi Park, and Charisse Pearlina Weston, in addition to a photographic print by Paul Strand.This exhibition takes cues from the resonant and material transformations that are present within the sonic, architectural, and abstract forms of Drozd’s installation—specifically, how these elements impact bodily experience.

Listening Party and

Artist-Led Tour

PUBLIC PROGRAM
Sat. October 15, 2–3:30PM

Join us for an intimate tour of the current exhibitions, led by exhibiting artists Luba Drozd, Leah Beeferman, and Mimi Park. First, Drozd will lead attendees through her solo exhibition,The Tenacity of a Fluid Trace, in which she employs elements of Smack Mellon’s unique architecture to produce various tonal frequencies for a complex visual and sonic installation. Attendees will have the opportunity to experience the exhibition from an embodied perspective, accompanied by a talk with the artist detailing her process, research, and use of materials. After the tour of the main gallery, artists Leah Beeferman and Mimi Park will lead a tour of their works in the back gallery, as part of the exhibition The Dissolution caus’d by Fire is in all Bodies, conceived to accompany Drozd’s installation. Similarly, their works engage with issues around perception, sonic resonance, and material transformation.

Image: Luba Drozd, The Tenacity of a Fluid Trace (in process installation detail). 2022.

Smack Mellon is a nonprofit arts organization located in DUMBO, Brooklyn. Smack Mellon’s mission is to nurture and support emerging, under-recognized mid-career, and women artists in the creation and exhibition of new work by providing exhibition opportunities, studio workspace, and access to equipment and technical assistance for the realization of ambitious projects.
